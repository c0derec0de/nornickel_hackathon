# Catholyte circulation in nickel electrowinning (international review, 2021)

In international practice of nickel electrowinning, catholyte circulation is typically maintained
at a higher rate than in domestic Russian plants. Reported optimal catholyte flow is 15–20 L/min
per cell at electrolyte temperature 55–65 °C and current density 200–240 A/m². Higher circulation
(around 18 L/min) is claimed to improve current efficiency up to 97% and reduce nickel dendrite
formation, contradicting the domestic recommendation of 8–12 L/min.

The experiment was carried out at an overseas pilot facility. Author: J. Anderson, expert in
electrometallurgy. Geography: world / non-RU.

Note: this finding on optimal catholyte flow rate contradicts the Russian internal review which
recommends 8–12 L/min as optimal.
